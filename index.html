The Problem

ADHD breaks most systems at the worst moment: under load.

Life-stage transitions don't just add complexity — they destabilize every workaround that was holding things together.

"What worked pre-kids collapses when routines become multi-threaded."
Trigger: Life-stage shift
"Planning isn't the issue. Starting, switching, returning is."

"Tools multiply. Cognitive load skyrockets. Self-trust erodes."
Trigger: System overload
"A single missed step cascades into household chaos."
Trigger: Single-point failure

This isn't a character flaw. It's a systems mismatch: variable executive capacity vs. time-sensitive external demands. The scaffolding that once worked was built for a different version of your life.

A systems intervention for executive dysfunction.

Science & Safety

Anti-slop by design: evidence governance built in.

The ADHD O/S doesn't make claims it can't support. The guardrails are part of the architecture, not a disclaimer we added afterward.

01 · Foundation

Grounded in Dr. Russell Barkley's research on ADHD as executive function and self-regulation impairment — the most evidence-backed framework available for adult ADHD intervention design.

02 · Claims hygiene

The system includes explicit claims governance: what we can say, what we can't, and when to refer out. No outcome promises that aren't backed by the literature. No slop.

03 · AI as governance layer

AI is a compression and personalization tool, operating within evidence constraints — not a replacement for care, clinical support, or human judgment.

FAQ

Questions worth answering honestly.

Is this therapy?
No. The ADHD O/S is non-clinical scaffolding. It doesn't diagnose, treat, or manage a clinical condition. If you need therapy or psychiatric support, we'll point you to appropriate resources — that's built into the governance model.
Do I need a formal ADHD diagnosis?
A diagnosis is recommended. The system is designed around the clinical reality of ADHD as executive dysfunction — it's more precise, and more honest, to work with people who share that baseline.
Is this another productivity app?
No. It's a protocol + coaching + minimal dashboard. The distinction matters: apps require activation energy to use. This is an execution layer designed to survive the conditions when apps get abandoned.
What if I've tried everything and quit?
That's expected. Re-entry is built in from the start — not as a feature you unlock later. The system assumes dropout. Restart paths are part of the architecture, not an afterthought.
How is this different from ADHD coaching?
Coaching without protocol is highly dependent on the individual coach and session continuity. The ADHD O/S is protocolized, artifact-producing, and governance-constrained — you build something you own and can operate between sessions.
How is this different from Inflow, Tiimo, or Notion?
Those are tools. This is an execution layer. A hammer doesn't tell you what to build or how to come back after you set it down. The ADHD O/S provides the protocol, the consistency engine, and the restart path — tools are optional components inside it.
What does the AI do in this system?
Compression and personalization — within evidence constraints. It helps adapt templates to your specific life stage and failure mode. It doesn't make clinical claims, provide therapy, or operate outside the Barkley-grounded guardrails.
